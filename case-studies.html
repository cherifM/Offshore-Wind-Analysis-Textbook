Wind Resource Assessment

  • Mean wind speed: 9.5 m/s at 100m
  • Weibull k parameter: 2.3
  • Capacity factor: 48%
  • Annual energy production: ~5.5 TWh
  • Met mast campaign: 3 years
  • LiDAR validation: 18 months

Foundation Design

  • Type: Monopiles
  • Diameter: 8-10 meters
  • Weight: up to 1,400 tonnes
  • Water depth: 23-28 meters
  • Scour protection: Rock placement
  • Installation vessel: specialized jack-up

Key Lessons Learned

  • Economies of scale: Larger turbines (8 MW vs 6 MW) reduced total number needed, lowering installation costs
  • Supply chain optimization: Coordinated manufacturing and installation reduced weather delays
  • Grid integration: Reactive power compensation equipment essential for stable operation at 89 km offshore
  • Environmental monitoring: Comprehensive marine mammal monitoring showed minimal impact during construction
Challenge: COVID-19 pandemic disrupted supply chains and offshore operations
Solution: Implemented strict health protocols, created offshore "bubbles", adjusted vessel rotations
Success: Achieved 35% cost reduction compared to earlier projects through technology and scale
Impact: Powers 1.4 million UK homes annually

Floating Technology Innovation

Spar-Buoy Design

  • Draft: 78 meters below surface
  • Displacement: 12,000 tonnes
  • Ballast: Iron ore and water
  • Mooring: 3-line catenary system
  • Anchor type: Suction anchors
  • Motion response: < 5° in extreme conditions

Performance Metrics

  • Availability: 97.5% (2018-2022 average)
  • Best month CF: 65% (February 2020)
  • Hurricane survival: Validated to 50-year storm
  • O&M optimization: Remote reset capabilities
  • Wake losses: < 5% due to spacing
  • Grid compliance: Full reactive power range

Floating Wind Insights

  • Stability validation: Motion compensation systems performed better than modeled
  • Maintenance access: Higher accessibility than fixed-bottom in similar conditions
  • Cost trajectory: Demonstrated path to cost parity with fixed-bottom by 2030
  • Environmental benefits: Minimal seabed disturbance, reversible installation

Regulatory and Market Insights

  • Permitting process: 8-year development timeline established framework for future projects
  • Local benefits: Replaced diesel generation on Block Island, reducing costs 40%
  • Supply chain: Demonstrated need for U.S. port infrastructure development
  • Environmental studies: Comprehensive 5-year pre/post construction monitoring program
  • Tourism impact: Became attraction, increasing island visits
Challenge: No existing U.S. offshore wind supply chain or installation vessels
Solution: Imported expertise and equipment from Europe, initiated U.S. vessel construction
